Problem

Colorism as a systemic issue that negatively affects people's access to opportunities, safety, and fair representation in technology.

Proctoring Technology

Proctoring tools like LockDown Browser + Respondus Monitor show bias against darker skin, often due to poor lighting. This results in frequent false alarms, unfairly penalizing students.

Self-Driving Cars

Self-driving cars struggle to spot dark-skinned pedestrians accurately, with detection rates about five percentage points lower. This issue raises safety concerns.

Mislabeling Black People as Gorillas

Google Photos once mistakenly tagged Black people as "gorillas," revealing serious AI bias. This highlighted the need for better, more inclusive technology.

Colorism

Racial discrimination is a pervasive problem in the USA. As defined by Harvard, colorism, or skin color stratification, is a process that privileges light-skinned people of color over dark.
